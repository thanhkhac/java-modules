    1.              Purpose

This SRS describes the functional and non-functional requirements for software release 1.0 of the Attendance System. This document is intended to be used by the members of the project team who will implement and verify the correct functioning of the system. Unless otherwise noted, all requirements specified here are committed for release 1.0.

1.3             Project Scope

The Attendance System is a software solution designed to automate and centralize the tracking of employee work hours and attendance within a company. The system's primary goals include providing accurate time tracking, attendance monitoring, compliance with labor regulations, a user-friendly interface, and reporting. This project aligns with business objectives such as operational efficiency, cost reduction, employee satisfaction, compliance adherence, and data-driven decision-making.

2.2             User Classes and Characteristics

Employee

Regular employees are the primary users of the timekeeping system. They need a user-friendly interface to log in and out, enter work hours, and submit leave requests, overtime requests. Their access may be limited to viewing their own records and managing personal information.

Management staff

As managers, they can view employees and approve applications for employees in their departments.

Department Manager

As department managers, they have the same functions as management staff. However, they can assign employee roles within their departments

HR

HR (Human Resources): Human Resources plays an important role in the timekeeping system with tasks such as employee management, access control, attendance tracking, and leave approval. They also ensure compliance with HR policies, create reports and analyze data
